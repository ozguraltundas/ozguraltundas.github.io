Research Overview

Özgür Altundaş is an agricultural genetic engineer and a Ph.D. candidate in Plant Breeding and Genetics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln. His doctoral studies are supported by the YLSY scholarship from the Republic of Türkiye. He has experience in both industry and academia, having contributed to plant breeding projects as well as research in plant physiology. His work in molecular plant breeding led to a patent application, reflecting his innovative approach to advancing the field.

Research Focus Areas

  • Quantitative trait loci (QTL) mapping in maize
  • Genomic prediction and trait dissection
  • Maize transformation and embryogenic callus formation
  • Integration of physiological and molecular data in breeding
