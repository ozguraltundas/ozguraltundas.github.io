About Me

I am an agricultural genetic engineer and a Ph.D. candidate in Plant Breeding and Genetics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, working in the Schnable Lab. My doctoral studies are supported by the YLSY Scholarship from the Republic of Türkiye. I have experience in both academia and industry, having contributed to plant breeding projects and research in plant physiology.

My research focuses on genetic dissection of complex traits and developing innovative approaches for enhancing crop productivity. I received awards such as the Social Engagement Award at the Bayer-UNL Hackathon, Second Place in the Elevator Speech Contest, and presented my work at the 15th Annual Plant Sciences Symposium (UW–Madison).

About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Plant Breeding and Genetics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, working in the Schnable Lab. My research focuses on quantitative genetics, QTL mapping, high-throughput phenotyping, and improving crop productivity under varying environmental conditions.

I work on large-scale data analysis including SpATS modeling, heritability estimation, GWAS, genotype–phenotype integration, and multi-location field trial evaluation in maize and sorghum.
