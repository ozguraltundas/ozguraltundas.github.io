About Me

Plant Breeding · Genetics · Physiology · High-Throughput Phenotyping

Who I Am

I am a Ph.D. student in Plant Breeding & Genetics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, working in the Schnable Lab. My research focuses on the genetic architecture of complex traits in maize and sorghum, integrating quantitative genetics, physiology, and computational workflows to understand plant performance across diverse environments.

Before starting my Ph.D., I completed my M.Sc. in Plant Biotechnology, where I worked on physiological responses to abiotic stresses and plant–microbe interactions. I have also contributed to several industry-driven crop improvement projects, particularly in tomato breeding, genetic biofortification, and molecular marker development.

I am supported by the YLSY Scholarship awarded by the Republic of Türkiye, a competitive fellowship supporting international graduate study and research.
