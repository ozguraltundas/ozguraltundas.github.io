About Me

If you are reading this, I believe we may share something in common — maybe you are a graduate student, a professor, a researcher, or one of my friends. In any case, I would like to tell you why I created this website.

I believe science should belong to everyone — not to a small group. It should be accessible, understandable, and shareable. This website is my small contribution to that idea. I want to share my work with you and support open, clear scientific communication.

Since my childhood, I have always been curious, wanting to understand every detail of how things work. That curiosity is probably why I chose to pursue my academic career abroad. I grew up in a village, in a farming family, surrounded by animals, plants, and nature. That is why I chose agriculture as my field of study.

Life was not always easy. Like everyone else, I experienced ups and downs. I worked many different jobs outside my academic interests — from hotel reception to agricultural labor and produce handling. But I also won Türkiye’s most prestigious fellowships twice, worked on national research projects, applied for a patent, won competitions, and was selected as “Employee of the Year.”

Today, I am a PhD student, hoping to deepen my skills and knowledge in plant breeding, plant genetics, and quantitative genomics. I am currently a member of the Schnable Lab at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln.

Outside of research, I love horse riding, reading books, playing football, walking, and giving presentations. I actually have a horse named Gece — my best friend. I even dedicated my master’s thesis to him.

If you'd like to learn more about my academic background, feel free to check my CV. But briefly:

  • BSc in Agricultural Engineering — Nigde Omer Halisdemir University
  • MSc in Plant Biotechnology — Gebze Technical University
  • PhD student in Agronomy & Horticulture — University of Nebraska–Lincoln
  • Researcher in the Schnable Lab
