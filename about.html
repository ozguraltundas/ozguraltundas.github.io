About Me

Plant Breeding, Genetics & Quantitative Genomics

Who I Am

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Plant Breeding & Genetics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, working in the Schnable Lab. My research integrates plant genomics, quantitative genetics, high-throughput phenotyping, and computational data analysis to understand trait variation and improve crop performance.

My academic background includes agricultural genetic engineering, plant biotechnology, and molecular breeding, supported by hands-on experience in both industry and research environments.

Research Interests

  • Quantitative genetics & multi-environment analysis
  • High-throughput field phenotyping & drone/ground sensing
  • Maize genomic selection and trait prediction
  • QTL mapping and candidate gene identification
  • Genotype–phenotype integration pipelines

Education

  • Ph.D., Plant Breeding & Genetics — University of Nebraska–Lincoln
  • M.S., Plant Biotechnology — Gebze Technical University
  • B.S., Agricultural Genetic Engineering — Niğde Ömer Halisdemir University

Beyond Research

I am passionate about scientific communication, data visualization, agricultural innovation, and helping bridge classical breeding with modern computational genomics.
