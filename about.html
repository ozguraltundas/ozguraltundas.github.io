About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Plant Breeding & Genetics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, working in the Schnable Lab under the supervision of Prof. James Schnable. My research focuses on maize genomics, quantitative genetics, and high-throughput phenotyping, with an emphasis on understanding the genetic architecture of complex agronomic traits.

I develop reproducible pipelines that integrate large-scale phenotypic datasets (multispectral, physiological, and yield-related traits) with genomic and environmental information. My goal is to dissect genotype–phenotype–environment interactions and identify the genetic factors that contribute to improved crop performance.

Before starting my Ph.D., I completed my M.S. degree in Plant Biotechnology and contributed to several national projects supported by TÜBİTAK. During this time, I gained experience in QTL mapping, GWAS, molecular characterization, tissue culture, and genotype evaluation. I have presented my work at multiple national and international conferences.

Research Interests

  • Quantitative genetics and multi-environment trait analysis
  • High-throughput phenotyping (multispectral, photosynthesis traits, UAV pipelines)
  • Genomic analyses, GWAS, QTL mapping and fine-mapping
  • Genotype–phenotype integration workflows
  • Data-driven crop improvement and prediction models
