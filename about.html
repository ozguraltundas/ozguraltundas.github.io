About Me

Plant Breeding, Genetics & Quantitative Genomics

Who I Am

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Plant Breeding & Genetics at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln, focusing on quantitative genomics, maize genetics, and high-throughput phenotyping. With a strong passion for integrating molecular biology, field phenotyping, and big-data analytics, my work aims to better understand genotype × environment interactions and develop improved plant traits.

Background

Before joining UNL, I worked as an Agricultural Genetic Engineer and completed my M.S. in Plant Biotechnology at Gebze Technical University. My academic path includes extensive research in plant physiology, molecular genetics, epigenetics, and plant–microbe interactions.

Research Interests

  • Maize transformation & embryogenic callus QTL
  • Genomic prediction & trait dissection
  • High-throughput phenotyping (HTP)
  • G × E modeling and mixed linear models
  • QTL mapping & GWAS pipelines

Education

  • PhD in Plant Breeding & Genetics — University of Nebraska–Lincoln (2025–Present)
  • M.S. in Plant Biotechnology — Gebze Technical University (2023)
  • B.S. in Agricultural Genetic Engineering — Niğde Ömer Halisdemir University (2020)
